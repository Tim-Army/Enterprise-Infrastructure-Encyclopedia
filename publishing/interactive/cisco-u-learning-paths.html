Source & method. Pulled from the Cisco U. public catalog API (api-learning.cisco.com · search-type-unauth, type = path) on 22 July 2026. CE credits is each path's number_of_credits; 0 means the path is not CE-eligible (typically no-lab / “lite” content). Access reflects lock_content — Free paths are open with a free Cisco U. account, Subscription paths require a paid plan; individual paths are not sold à la carte here. Exam / cert is the exam (code + acronym) or certification a path prepares you for. Most values come from Cisco’s catalog tagging; five courses it left untagged or stale-tagged were filled from verified current sources — 350-101 WLCOR (wireless core), 300-110 WLSD and 300-120 WLSI (wireless design and implementation), 300-620 DCACI (ACI), and 300-710 SNCF (advanced firewall). Cisco split wireless into its own CCNP Wireless track on 19 March 2026: WLCOR replaced ENCOR as its core, and WLSD/WLSI are the realigned successors of the retired 300-425 ENWLSD and 300-430 ENWLSI, which the two “Enterprise Wireless” paths were built for. “—” means no current exam maps; the Collaboration Applications/Conferencing courses are left blank because their concentration exams (e.g., CLICA 300-810) are retiring in 2026. Some tracks (AWS, Azure, Microsoft, Kubernetes, and other non-Cisco content) are hosted on Cisco U. and carry 0 CE credits. Catalog contents, credit values, and pricing change often — verify on the path page and submit credits at ce.cisco.com before relying on a number.
