# Volume XXXII Glossary

Definitions for terms introduced in **Volume XXXII — Dell Technologies
Certification Tracks**, alphabetized. See also the
[volume index](INDEX.md) and the [master glossary](../../GLOSSARY.md).

**D-code** — Dell's exam identifier grammar,
`D-<product>-<function>-<series>`: function FN/OE/DY/DS/IN/MN, with
`-A-` marking achievement-style credentials. Introduced in Chapter 01.

**DD Boost** — Data Domain's client-side deduplication protocol,
moving dedupe work to the backup source and enabling managed file
replication. Introduced in Chapter 06.

**MTree** — A Data Domain managed tree: the unit of tenancy,
replication, and Retention Lock on the filesystem. Introduced in
Chapter 06.

**OneFS** — PowerScale's operating system, fusing all nodes into a
single filesystem and namespace with per-policy erasure protection.
Introduced in Chapter 04.

**Protection domain (PowerFlex)** — The PowerFlex failure boundary
grouping SDS nodes; paired with storage pools to bound rebuild and
performance blast radius. Introduced in Chapter 05.

**Retention Lock** — Data Domain immutability (governance and
compliance modes); the vault doctrine's enforcement mechanism.
Introduced in Chapter 06.

**SmartFabric** — PowerSwitch mode in which an owning appliance
(VxRail, MX) manages the fabric as a system rather than per-switch
CLI. Introduced in Chapter 07.

**SRDF** — Symmetrix Remote Data Facility: PowerMax's replication
family (synchronous, asynchronous, Metro active-active). Introduced
in Chapter 04.

**SnapVX** — PowerMax's snapshot facility, policy-driven and
storage-group scoped. Introduced in Chapter 04.

**VLT (Virtual Link Trunking)** — OS10's active-active dual-homing
between a switch pair; one failure domain wearing two boxes.
Introduced in Chapter 07.

**vVols storage container** — PowerStore's VMware-native provisioning
surface, replacing datastore-level abstraction with per-VM volumes.
Introduced in Chapter 03.

**XE series** — PowerEdge's accelerated line (GPU-dense, advanced
cooling) carrying Dell's AI infrastructure; certified by its own
Install/Operate pair. Introduced in Chapter 02.
