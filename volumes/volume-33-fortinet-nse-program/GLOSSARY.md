# Volume XXXIII Glossary

Definitions for terms introduced in **Volume XXXIII — Fortinet NSE
Program**, alphabetized. See also the [volume index](INDEX.md) and the
[master glossary](../../GLOSSARY.md) for cross-volume terminology.

**FortiCNAPP** — Fortinet's cloud-native application protection
platform: posture (CSPM), workload (CWPP), IaC, and runtime security
for cloud estates. Introduced in Chapter 04.

**FortiLink** — The protocol and mode by which a FortiGate manages
FortiSwitch and FortiAP as extensions of its own control plane —
one policy point for wired and wireless access. Introduced in
Chapter 02.

**FortiSASE** — Fortinet's cloud-delivered SASE service (secure web
gateway, ZTNA, CASB, firewall-as-a-service) for users off the branch,
enforcing one policy with Secure SD-WAN. Introduced in Chapter 05.

**NSE (Network Security Expert)** — Fortinet's certification program,
restored on 15 July 2026 to an eight-level (NSE 1–8) numbered
progression across four tracks. Introduced in Chapter 01.

**NSE 8** — The expert tier: an NSE 8 Core practical exam module plus
one NSE 8 Specialization practical (within one year of the core),
valid two years. Introduced in Chapter 06.

**Security Fabric** — Fortinet's integrated architecture in which
products (FortiGate, FortiAnalyzer, FortiEDR, FortiSOAR, and others)
share telemetry and coordinate enforcement. Introduced in Chapter 03.

**Track** — One of the four NSE certification specializations — Secure
Networking, Security Operations, Cloud Security, SASE — that determine
which exam a given NSE level represents. Introduced in Chapter 01.

**ZTNA (Zero Trust Network Access)** — Identity- and posture-aware
application access, replacing broad VPN, powered on the endpoint by
FortiClient EMS and delivered through FortiSASE. Introduced in
Chapter 05.
