# Chapter 07: Appendix — VMware and Broadcom Certifications and Course Access

Every current certification in the VMware by Broadcom program, organized
by tier, with its exam code, any announced exam end date, and the
official training that prepares it. Every exam code and every retirement
date below was harvested on **23 July 2026** from Broadcom's own
certification pages — read from the `getjson` education-program API
endpoint each page injects, because the raw page HTML omits the code —
the same harvest that anchors
[Volume V — VMware Virtualization](../../volume-05-vmware-virtualization/README.md),
whose Chapters 12–20 map this program to hands-on content.

**How access works at Broadcom.** VMware's model is
certification-first and mostly paid. **Instructor-led and on-demand
training** lives on **Learning@Broadcom** (formerly VMware Customer
Connect Learning); courses are paid, and each certification page names
its recommended course(s). **Exams** are delivered through Broadcom's
authorized testing partner (fees apply). The one large free resource is
**[VMware Hands-on Labs](https://labs.hol.vmware.com/HOL/catalog)** —
browser-based, no licensing — plus nested ESXi under the free-for-personal-use
VMware Workstation/Fusion. Note the licensing order: personal-use product
licenses (via VMUG Advantage) are now released by *passing* a VCP, so
they are a reward for certifying, not a route to preparing. The
Distinguished Expert (VCDX) is earned by a design defense, not a course
or a written exam.

> **Currency.** This is the fastest-churning program in this
> encyclopedia — the Broadcom acquisition renamed VCDX and restructured
> VCP around VVF/VCF, and VMware Cloud Foundation 9.0 introduced an
> entire new advanced (VCAP) role series. **Two exams are retiring on 31
> July 2026** (see the Exam end date column). Confirm each exam's code,
> its recommended course, and its availability on the certification's
> Broadcom page before registering or purchasing. Volume V, Chapter 20
> defines the recurring currency check that keeps this table honest.

## Free training and entry points

- **[VMware Hands-on Labs](https://labs.hol.vmware.com/HOL/catalog)** —
  free, browser-based, guided and (Odyssey) unguided labs; the closest
  substitute for a licensed home lab (Volume V README maps the catalog)
- **VMware Workstation / Fusion** — free for personal use; run nested
  ESXi for the build labs Hands-on Labs does not cover
- **Official exam guides** — free downloads on Learning@Broadcom; the
  authority on each blueprint's testable objectives
- Everything under **paid** below is Learning@Broadcom instructor-led or
  on-demand training

## Reading the exam-code families

The exam number's prefix signals the tier and scope, which is worth
knowing before opening any exam guide:

- **`2V0`** — mainstream professional (VCP): broad, role-defining
- **`6V0`** — specialist professional: a narrow single-product VCP (Avi,
  Private Cloud Security)
- **`3V0`** — advanced (VCAP): advanced design or deploy depth
- **`5V0`** — specialist skills: focused skills exams for one solution
  area (Telco Cloud)

## Notes on the training column

- Course versions do not always match exam versions. The VVF/VCF exams
  target 9.0 with matching V9.0 courses; VCP-NV tests NSX 4.x against V4.0
  and V4.x courses; VCP-DCV tests the vSphere 8 generation. Where a course
  version leads or trails its exam, the blueprint remains the authority on
  what is testable.
- The two Support exams (2V0-15.25 and 2V0-18.25) share one course —
  *VMware Cloud Foundation: Troubleshooting [V9.0]* — which makes taking
  them close together efficient.
- Course names are as published on each certification's Broadcom page at
  the harvest date; confirm the current course and its price at
  enrollment, since Learning@Broadcom course pages require a login and the
  lineup changes.
