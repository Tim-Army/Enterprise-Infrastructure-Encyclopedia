# Chapter 10: Appendix — Google Cloud Certifications and Course Access

Every current Google Cloud certification, organized by level, with its
exam format, fee, validity period, any announced exam end date, and the
official training that prepares it. Every fact below was harvested on
**23 July 2026** from Google Cloud's own certification pages — the same
harvest that anchors
[Volume XXXIV — Google Cloud Certification Tracks](../../volume-34-google-cloud-certifications/README.md),
whose nine chapters map this program to hands-on content.

**How access works at Google.** **[Google Skills](https://cloud.google.com/learn)**
provides a **free learning path for every certification below**, with
on-demand courses and hands-on labs. Professional Cloud Architect
additionally publishes its **case studies in advance** on the exam guide —
free preparation that many candidates skip. **Exams are paid** and booked
through Google's certification pages and its proctoring partner, delivered
**online-proctored or onsite**.

**Recertification is a full paid retake.** There is no free renewal
assessment of the kind Microsoft offers, so validity periods are a
recurring budget item: roughly **$100 per year** to hold a professional
certification against **$42** for an associate.

> **Currency.** Google Cloud is the only major cloud program with **no
> exam codes** — certifications are named credentials, so a re-scope,
> rename, or move between levels leaves **no version signal**. Google's
> certification page currently states exams are being updated for
> **Google Cloud Next '26**, naming the **Gemini Enterprise Agent
> Platform** and **Google Cloud's data and analytics stack**, and directs
> candidates to each exam guide for products actually covered. Confirm any
> certification on its own page before registering. Volume XXXIV,
> Chapter 09 defines the recurring currency check.

## Why the exam-code column reads "none"

Google Cloud is alone among the major cloud programs in **not using exam
codes**. There is no equivalent of AWS's `SAA-C03` or Azure's `AZ-104`; a
certification is identified solely by name.

That removes the fastest currency signal the other programs provide. In
this encyclopedia's AWS and Azure appendices, a version suffix immediately
flags stale study material. Here, checking currency means:

- confirming the certification **still appears** on Google's certification
  page, **at the level you expect**;
- comparing the **exam guide's section list** against your study material;
- reading any **program notice**, such as the Next '26 refresh.

A re-levelled certification — Google Workspace Administrator now sits at
associate level — leaves no trace in its name.

## Cost of holding a certification

Because recertification is a full retake, validity is a recurring cost:

| Level | Fee | Validity | Approximate cost per year |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Foundational | $99 | 3 years | $33 |
| Associate | $125 | 3 years | $42 |
| Professional | $200 | 2 years | **$100** |

The professional tier is the shortest-lived and most expensive — roughly
three times the annual cost of an associate credential. Hold the ones the
role actually uses.

## Notes

- **No prerequisites anywhere**, including at professional level. Google
  states recommended experience instead — typically three or more years of
  industry experience with one or more on Google Cloud for professional
  certifications.
- **Fees exclude tax** and are shown in USD; regional pricing may differ at
  registration.
- **The Next '26 refresh is a content revision, not a retirement.** No
  current Google Cloud certification carries a published end date, which is
  why every row above reads "—".
- **Google Workspace Administrator covers a different product** — the
  collaboration suite, not Google Cloud Platform — despite sitting in the
  same certification program.
